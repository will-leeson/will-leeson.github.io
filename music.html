Music

My greatest joy in life is music. Some would say I listen an "unhealthy amount", but I can't hear them because I'm jamming out to some Bowie. I listen to just about anything, but my personal favorites are folk, progressive rock, and new wave.

Playing

    I love to play music. I grew up as a drummer. I took concert lessons in middle school and eventually I took set lessons for a couple years. Although I play far less, I still consider myself a drummer at heart. My favorite drummers are Stewart Copeland, Neil Peart, and Phil Collins.
    When I got to college, I was feeling the itch to learn guitar. Instead of jumping straight into it, I decided to pick up the ukulele. I spent a year playing it before moving onto guitar. Now I find myself playing a lot of folk music, like Neil Young, Harry Chapin, and Dawes, and loving every minute of it. My goals are to eventually take up the piano again and to learn the banjo so I can play like Steve Martin.

Concerts

  Much to the dismay of my bank account, I love going to see live music. The first concert I saw was Rush when I was in high school and I've never looked back. I like to see the older stars before they stop touring. So far I've caught Billy Joel, Bob Dylan, The Who, Paul McCartney, ELO, Steely Dan, and KISS. Some of my favorite shows have been Dawes, Rush, TWRP, and KISS. The weirdest show I've seen was Bob Dylan. He played 20 songs, I didn't understand a word he said, and he never once acknowledged the audience. I highly recommend seeing him.
